What does Cale do?

Cale specializes in helping students plan and create outstanding applications for college admissions and scholarships. He saves families time and money while teaching his students life-long skills that matter.

Students and parents always have a million things going on when senior year hits, but the window of opportunity is only a few short months long. If you don't know exactly what to do and how to do it you are leaving money on the table. There is so much clutter on the internet that it makes it very hard to know what is worth your time and even harder to know the best way to do it. But if you miss either one of these points you are foregoing opportunity, especially with scholarships.

Cale removes the clutter and gives his students precise step-by-step instruction. Specifically, he provides unprecedented value during essay planning and letters of recommendation. Most students write their essays around shallow, generic ideas and Cale knows how to draw out inspiring, deep ideas from his students. Even if you have the best stories, it takes +10 hours of revising and editing to turn them into incredible essays. Starting every essay with the right ideas is essential.

Why does Cale do what he does?

Working with students to optimize their applications is the greatest vessel to teach critical life and technical skills that Cale's students take with them for the rest of their lives. In school the primary external motivator is GPA; that vessel often isn't strong enough to motivate learning something. You can have a unit in school on how to write a resume or write a persuasive essay. But when the stakes are high and an outstanding applications are what stands between getting into Harvard, graduating debt free, or getting a job offer at Google, the situation changes. Students now want to write the best resume, the essay, and have the best interview.

Cale loves working with students during these high stakes times because it gives him the opportunity to change a student's beliefs about what is possible about themselves. To him it isn't only about getting accepted, getting the offer, or earning the scholarship. Even with the best applications it comes down to luck in the end. Cale loves the work he does because he can bring out persistance, optimism, confidence, and good ethics. Cale loves the work he does because when the pressure is on he can help turn his students into the best versions of themselves. Hearing the news when a student receives a $10,000 scholarship, was accepted to their target university, or got that job offer is incredible. But the skills learned and mastered from working with Cale never go away. Concise and persuasive writing, managing deadlines, leveraging your strengths and outsourcing weaknesses in a team setting, strategic thinking and decision making. These skills will be used over and over in students professional lives and the sooner they master them, the more opportunities they will be exposed to.
